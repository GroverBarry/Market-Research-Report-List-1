<p><p>The ophthalmic guidewire market encompasses two main types: stainless steel and nitinol. Stainless steel guidewires are made from a high-strength steel alloy, known for its durability and resistance to corrosion. They are commonly used in ophthalmic procedures to navigate delicate areas with precision. On the other hand, nitinol guidewires are crafted from a shape-memory alloy that can retain its shape after being bent or twisted. Nitinol guidewires are flexible, allowing for easier maneuverability in intricate ophthalmic surgeries. Both stainless steel and nitinol guidewires play vital roles in ensuring successful and accurate ophthalmic procedures.</p></p>
