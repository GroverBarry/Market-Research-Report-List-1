<p><p>The global Toxoplasmosis Diagnostic Testing Market is projected to experience significant growth over the forecast period of 2022-2028. Toxoplasmosis is a parasitic disease caused by the Toxoplasma gondii parasite, which can be transmitted through the consumption of contaminated meat, exposure to infected cat feces, or congenitally from a mother to her unborn child. The rising prevalence of toxoplasmosis worldwide is driving the demand for effective diagnostic testing methods.</p><p>Currently, the market is dominated by serological tests, such as enzyme immunoassays (EIAs) and immunoblotting, which detect the presence of antibodies in the blood. These tests are widely used due to their non-invasive nature and high sensitivity. However, there is a growing demand for more accurate and rapid diagnostic solutions, leading to the development of molecular diagnostic tests, such as polymerase chain reaction (PCR) and nucleic acid amplification tests (NAATs). These tests offer improved specificity and can detect the presence of the parasite's DNA.</p><p>The increasing awareness about the adverse effects of toxoplasmosis and the availability of advanced diagnostic technologies are expected to drive the growth of the market.
