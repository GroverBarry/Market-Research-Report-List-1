<p><p>The non-tire synthetic rubber market comprises two main types: general rubber and special rubber. General rubber refers to the commonly used synthetic rubbers, such as styrene-butadiene rubber (SBR) and polybutadiene rubber (BR), which find broad applications in various industries. On the other hand, special rubber includes specialized types like nitrile rubber (NBR), ethylene propylene diene monomer (EPDM) rubber, and silicone rubber. Special rubber is tailored for specific applications, such as automotive parts, electrical insulation, and medical equipment, offering unique properties and performance characteristics to meet specific industry requirements.</p></p>
