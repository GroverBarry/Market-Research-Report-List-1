<p><p>The synthetic lubricants and functional fluids market is classified into three main types: Group III, Group IV, and Group V. Group III synthetic lubricants are made from severely hydrotreated base stocks, offering improved performance compared to conventional mineral oils. Group IV consists of true synthetic oils known as polyalphaolefins (PAO) that provide excellent temperature resistance and oxidative stability. Lastly, Group V includes all other synthetic oils, such as esters and polyalkylene glycols, known for their exceptional lubricity and compatibility with different materials. These classifications help consumers choose lubricants based on specific needs and performance requirements.</p></p>
