<p><p>Electronic Toll Collection (ETC) Systems are advanced technology systems that enable the automation of toll payment processes on highways and bridges. <p><p>Electronic Toll Collection (ETC) Systems are used to automate toll collection on highways and roads, reducing congestion and improving traffic flow. There are three main types of ETC systems:</p><p>1. Vehicle Automatic Understanding System: This type of ETC system uses sensors installed on toll booths to detect and identify vehicles passing through. It automatically records the toll and deducts it from the driver's account.</p><p>2. Short Range Communication: This ETC system uses radio frequency identification (RFID) or dedicated short-range communication (DSRC) technology to communicate between the toll booth and the vehicle. The toll amount is deducted from a pre-paid account.</p><p>3. Global Position Finding Satellite System: This ETC system uses global positioning system (GPS) technology to track and locate vehicles. It calculates toll fees based on the vehicle's distance traveled or the specific toll road used, and deducts the amount accordingly.</p></p>
