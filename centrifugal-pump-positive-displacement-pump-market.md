They fall into two types based on flow direction: axial flow pumps (fluid flows parallel to the pump shaft), and mixed or radial flow pumps (fluid flows at an angle to the pump shaft). Positive displacement pumps, on the other hand, have a different operating principle. They move fluids by trapping a fixed amount and then forcing it into the discharge pipe. Peripheral pumps and jet pumps are two types of positive displacement pumps.</p></p>
