<p><p>The custom dry ingredients blend market types consist of four categories: flake, flour, bran, and whole form. Flake refers to finely processed dry ingredients that have been flattened, such as rolled oats or cereal flakes. Flour includes finely ground dry ingredients, mainly derived from grains or legumes, like wheat or chickpea flour. Bran denotes the outer layers of grains, which are rich in fiber and often used as an ingredient or additive. Lastly, whole form refers to dry ingredients that are kept intact, such as whole grains or seeds, which are commonly used for baking or cooking purposes.</p></p>
